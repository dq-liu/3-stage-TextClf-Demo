Even during the Great Recession , Karl Lagerfeld spent liberally on his sets for Chanel shows at each Paris fashion week . There was the iceberg , a 28-foot frozen mountain purportedly hacked from a glacier , hauled to France from Sweden in 15 tractor-trailers and then trucked back again . There was the supermarket , a fake food hall complete with Chanel-labeled products , which was ransacked by guests ( and at which , Joan Smalls pushed Cara Delevingne and Rihanna in a cart ) . There was the staged protest ; a manufactured beach , complete with swelling waters ; and a giant bouclé jacket sculpture , in the most literal homage to the Chanel ’ s storied brand . And who could forget the rocket ship that actually took off ? Mr. Lagerfeld ’ s legacy is his visual identity , which was also expressed through these sets . They seemed to get bigger and bigger with every passing year .
